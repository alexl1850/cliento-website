The honest take

Different tools, different jobs

Wix is a legitimately good product if what you want is a website builder — the editor is polished, the templates are solid, and if you like designing things, you'll enjoy using it. That's not what Akus is trying to compete on.

Akus assumes you don't want to spend your Saturday dragging elements around a canvas. You answer a few questions about your business once, and you get a finished website — plus everything that normally comes after the website: social content, email campaigns, blog posts, and review management, none of which Wix touches on its own.
